Instead, she gave us a book to read and learn from. Even though I did not agree with the teaching style, this class still became one of my all-time favourite classes in high-school. I was always interested in making my own game and apps, so I taught myself the language and immediately fell in love with programming. I remember making my first text-based game with graphics and a fitness app. These projects weren’t crazy complex or anything, but they got me hooked on coding and they are the reason I decided to pursue Software Engineering.

IEEE Women In Engineering

IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) has been my passion project for the past two years. After noticing I was one of the 10 girls in my class of 150 students, I wanted to take this opportunity to celebrate diversity, promote inclusion, meet more women in my field, and make IEEE WIE more well-known in the community.

IEEE WIE had only hosted an annual Wine and Cheese networking event with IEEE WIE uOttawa for the past 9 years, but the IEEE WIE Chair at the time and I wanted to change that in 2018/2019. With very limited funds and only the two of us planning events, we went from hosting 1 joint event per year to hosting 5 successful events in the school year. The attendance to the Wine and Cheese increased from around 100 attendees to 150! We were able to form a strong connection with faculty, students, women in the industry, and the Women in Computer Science society.

I ran for the IEEE WIE Chair for the 2019/2020 school year and took the IEEE WIE affinity group to a whole new level. The new Vice-Chair and I hosted events with other engineering clubs, other women-oriented clubs, and with a ton more industry professionals, allowing IEEE WIE Carleton to become much more well known in the community. We hosted 7 events and increased student participation from ~10-20 to ~35-50 attendees per event! To add, I acted as the co-lead 2020 IEEE WIE Wine and Cheese and was able to get 13 companies to sponsor and fully fund the event! I was also nominated and won the IEEE Canadian Foundation scholarship for my continuous efforts and contributions to the Carleton branch.

Shopify heavily emphasizes the importance of diversity and inclusion in their company culture and it is something I strongly believe in as well. Participating in Technovation, a tech entrepreneurship competition founded by IBM and Shopify, was a defining moment for me in high-school. It gave me confidence and inspired me to continue in tech which was a gift I strive to give back to our community.

CuHacking

In January 2020, I attended my first ever hackathon! I teamed up with two of my friends and decided that we wanted to focus on creating a strong UI/UX for our hack. While brainstorming for ideas, I remembered hearing a lot of my friends complain about how annoying it was to find a subletter or find a new apartment in our area, so I came up with the idea of creating a website that brought together all of the apartment listings in a specified area. We scraped data from Kijiji in real-time, displayed the important information, and included a rating that was calculated using ML to compare it to other similar listings in the area. We started out designing the layout of the listings by creating a prototype using Figma. Once that was done, I started helping out with the front-end. Before this, I had never used React.js, so I was learning as I go. Even though I was very new to every aspect of this project, we completed our hack and won the Best UI/UX award!
